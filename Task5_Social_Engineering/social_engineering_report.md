# Social Engineering Attacks Report

## Introduction

Social engineering is a cybersecurity attack technique where attackers manipulate people to gain confidential information, access systems, or perform unauthorized actions.

Unlike technical attacks, social engineering focuses on exploiting human behavior and trust.

---

# Types of Social Engineering Attacks

## 1. Phishing

Phishing is a technique where attackers send fake emails, messages, or websites to trick users into revealing sensitive information.

Examples:
- Fake banking emails
- Fake login pages
- Malicious attachments


## 2. Spear Phishing

Spear phishing is a targeted phishing attack aimed at a specific person or organization.

Attackers usually collect information about the target before sending customized messages.


## 3. Pretexting

Pretexting involves creating a fake situation or identity to convince victims to share information.

Example:
An attacker pretending to be a company employee requesting account details.


## 4. Baiting

Baiting uses attractive offers or fake resources to trick users.

Examples:
- Free software downloads
- Infected USB devices


## 5. Tailgating

Tailgating occurs when an unauthorized person gains physical access by following an authorized person into a restricted area.


# Real World Examples

## Uber Social Engineering Attack (2022)

Attackers used social engineering techniques to convince an employee to approve access requests, which resulted in unauthorized access to internal systems.


## Twitter Attack (2020)

Attackers targeted employees through social engineering and gained access to internal tools.


# Impact of Social Engineering

- Data theft
- Financial loss
- Unauthorized access
- Reputation damage
- Privacy breaches


# Prevention Measures

## User Awareness Training

Employees should learn how to identify suspicious messages and requests.


## Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA adds additional security verification steps.


## Verify Requests

Users should verify unusual requests before sharing sensitive information.


## Email Security

Organizations should use spam filters and email protection systems.


# Conclusion

Social engineering remains one of the major cybersecurity threats because attackers target human behavior. Awareness, verification, and security practices help reduce these risks.
